# Story to Substance (STS)

## Overview

Story to Substance (STS) is an AI execution strategy for translating a target story into concrete, executable work. It takes a piece or pool of material and correlates the ideas within it against a target story, then progressively refines that correlation into a single actionable objective — which is either executed directly or broken down into discrete tasks before execution.

STS bridges the gap between narrative intent and grounded output by anchoring diffuse material to a story, collapsing it into a unified objective, and routing that objective through the most appropriate execution path.

---

## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Story** | The target narrative, goal, or intent that guides the entire strategy — the "what we are trying to capture" |
| **Material** | A single piece of content or a pool of multiple pieces used as source input |
| **Correlation** | The mapping of ideas found in the material to the elements or requirements of the story |
| **Objective** | The single unified goal produced by summarization — a distillation of all correlated material |
| **Direct path** | The execution route taken when the objective is concrete enough to be acted on without further decomposition |
| **Breakdown** | The decomposition of an objective into multiple discrete, executable tasks |
| **Tasks** | The one or more units of work that are ultimately executed — either a single objective on the direct path, or the itemized tasks produced by breakdown |
| **Substance** | The final output produced by executing the tasks — the grounded, realized form of the original story |

---

## Phases

### Phase 1: Correlation

Analyze the material — whether a single piece or a pool — and identify the ideas present within it. Map those ideas to the elements of the target story, establishing which parts of the material are relevant and how they relate to what the story is trying to capture.

- The story acts as a lens: material that doesn't map to the story is set aside
- Material that partially maps may be included with caveats
- The result of this phase is a correlated set of ideas anchored to the story

---

### Phase 2: Summarization

Take all correlated material from Phase 1 and link the ideas together into a coherent whole. Recompose them into a **single objective** — a clear, unified statement of what needs to be done to realize the story.

- All relevant material feeds into this phase; nothing correlated in Phase 1 is discarded
- The objective is the distilled form of the story as grounded by the material
- The objective should be specific enough to be assessed and acted on

---

### Phase 3: Assessment

Evaluate the objective produced in Phase 2 to determine the appropriate execution route.

- If the objective is **focused and directly executable**, route to the **direct path** — proceed immediately to Phase 5
- If the objective is **broad, compound, or complex**, route to **breakdown** — proceed to Phase 4 before execution

The assessment criteria center on scope and atomicity: can the objective be meaningfully completed as a single unit of work?

---

### Phase 4: Breakdown *(conditional)*

Decompose the objective into multiple discrete tasks. Each task should represent a self-contained unit of work that contributes to fulfilling the objective.

- Only entered if the assessment in Phase 3 determines breakdown is necessary
- Tasks should be itemized clearly, with each one independently executable
- The set of tasks, taken together, should fully cover the objective

---

### Phase 5: Execution

Execute the work — either the single objective (direct path) or the full set of tasks produced by breakdown. The output of this phase is **substance**: the realized, grounded form of the original story.

- On the direct path, execution acts on the objective itself
- On the breakdown path, execution acts on each itemized task
- Execution produces the final deliverable: the story captured as concrete output

---

      │
      ▼ Phase 3: Assessment
      ├─── focused / atomic ──────────────────────────┐
